Tommaso Bertagnin

2012 - INSTALLATION

WARKA

Water-gathering architecture for developing countries

 
 

The name Warka comes from a native tree of Ethiopia, where its shade offers a traditional public gathering place, a point of reunion and learning for the communities. Inspired by this role, Warka gathers solar energy during the day and water at night, becoming a precious support for the local inhabitants.

My contribution - I joined this project during the summer workshops at IUAV, Venice. The concept was almost completed, but for the first week I worked at the definition of possible interaction with the installation, and generated ideas for the lighting. The following week we built the 8.5m tall structure, I soldered the metal frame that keeps in shape the textile, and assembled the structure with the rest of the team.
