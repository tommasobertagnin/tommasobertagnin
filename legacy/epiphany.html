2013 - APPLICATION DESIGN

EPIPHANY

A moment of sudden revelation

 
 

Venice is a city with so many hidden things to experience that is hard to find them in a short period of time. Our app, Epiphany, aims to help people share with other users their “special moments” in the city, which can be experienced again when the same conditions occur. We want to encourage people to go out and “live” the city, rather than just looking at other people’s experiences through an app. Sensors are used to determine the specific state of the city when an experience is shared, and to check which experiences are available at the time someone uses the app.

Concept - An epiphany is a discovery. It applies to any situation in which an intense emotional experience, given in this case by a place, allows it to be understood from a new and deeper perspective. The surprising and fulfilling feeling of our app, Epiphany, is because one cannot predict when an experience will bear fruit, and our subconscious can play a significant part in delivering the solution. Epiphany loves exploration and helps motivated and curious people to share with other users their special moments lived in the city.

Users and Inhabitants - To better understand how Venice changes in relation to time, weather and season, we interviewed some inhabitants of the city. They confirmed that Venice is always beautiful, but there are some spots that become truly amazing in certain conditions. (NOTE: English subtitles available in the video controls bar)

My Contribution - Since the concept generation phase, I did a lot of research and proposed different directions. Moving to the design of the interface, I created the graphic solution that has been later used in the final application. I programmed the interactions with the cursor for the navigation section, the compass function, and structured the whole code coming from the rest of the team. Finally, I was the character in the video scenario, and also shoot some of the time-lapses in Venice as well as filming some of the interviews.
