Tommaso Bertagnin

2011 - SCREEN INTERACTION

HEDERA

Creative shadows in the silent night

 
 

With Hedera a group of artists are able to plan collective artistic performances using two relevant medium: nature and cities. People may create new and unusual green experiences, to sensitize public awareness about environment, in a new ethical and aesthetical view. Hedera is a surprising and exciting means of self expression, a mere pretext to provoke, think and debate. It discovers inspiration in an artistic context, but also raises issues related to environmental degradation and the lack of green areas, a pretext to provoke think and debate. Hedera - Venice is the perfect venue for this application: a “built on water” city with open squares (campi) and countless narrow streets (calli) full of open spaces, narrow routes and hidden alleys. In spite of these traits it’s not easy to find green areas. The aim was then to surprise the citizens with the sight, for example, of a simple grass lawn in a place where they would never have expected it.

What it does - Transform your mobile device into a creative tool of expression with Hedera! This application has two different modes: one for day, the other for night. During the day it’s all about preparing. You can choose to create, share or accept green missions. When the sun goes down and the moon appears, it is time for action. “Green soldiers” work in groups using Hedera’s powerful ability to capture CCTV camera views, with the sensors on their mobile devices they can communicate with hand gestures in a beautiful and effective way.

My contribution - During the initial phase of the project, I researched possible technologies to be implemented, in particular CCTVs and their use as observation points to control the action. After that I went on to find a solution for the drawing feature, and proposed graphic templates addressing the problem of representing a complex idea with a small screen interaction. As a complement to that, I designed other screens to show the overall mood of Hedera. In the development phase, I coded the circular main menu in Processing, as well as solving a few problems with the general flow of Hedera. In the final stages of the process, I was involved with shooting the night videos.
