Tommaso Bertagnin

2012 - DATA VIZ

SHAPE OF STORIES

Visualize how stories are told

 

 

This is a visual experiment with the aim of extracting a shape from how a story is narrated. Thanks to the structure of the English language, by searching in a phrase the use of personal pronouns, it's possible to understand if the narration points inwards or outwards therefore creating a visual pattern easily comprehensible.

Data visualisation - The dataset is a collection of .txt files, well known books, that are parsed by Processing to count the occurrencies of personal pronouns on each page. If a page has a predominance of one pronouns over the others, a coloured dot  is drawn and scaled proportionally to the occurencies. Each gray line represent a page, dots are then arranged along the wheel on three orders to emphasise the distance from the first person narration.

Thoughts - Far from being a precise visualisation, this was more an exploration on the methods and the processes of extracting meaningful information from a dataset. This is the result of a one week workshop, where I discovered the use of Processing for this kind of work, from the initial data mining to the final representation.
